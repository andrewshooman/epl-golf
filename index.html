How scoring works

Big picture: Everyone fills a 1–20 table (who wins, who goes down, etc.). We line that up with the real Premier League order whenever you hit refresh. Add up each club’s score — lowest total wins.

For each club on your sheet, we do two simple steps:

  1. Did you nail the exact place? Then that pick is −2 (that’s a “thank you” for being spot on). If not, that pick starts as how many places you were wrong by — e.g. you said 4th and they’re 7th → that’s 3.
  2. Does that row have an extra “zone” rule? (Same idea as certain rows in the workbook.) If yes, and the club actually lands in that zone, we take one more point off that pick (−1 again). The only zones we use are:
    • Top five — they really finish 1st through 5th
    • Sixth only — they really finish 6th
    • Seventh only — they really finish 7th
    • Bottom three — they really finish 18th, 19th, or 20th
    If your row doesn’t use one of those rules, we skip this step — only the number from step 1 counts.

Your score for one club = step 1 + step 2 (when step 2 applies). Your name in the leaderboard = all 20 clubs added together.

Extra columns: Perfect is how many clubs you got exactly right; 1 Away is how many were only one place off — handy to brag with, but the leaderboard still uses the math above.
